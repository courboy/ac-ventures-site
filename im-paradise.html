Executive Summary
Investment Thesis: Acquire a newly completed, purpose-built food manufacturing facility in Singapore's established Defu industrial estate via sale-leaseback with Paradise Group, a blue-chip F&B operator with 167 outlets across 22 cities. The 10-year absolute net lease with 1.5% annual escalations provides stable, bond-like income at an attractive 6.4% entry yield, compensating for the shorter JTC lease term.
Key Investment Highlights
  • Newly Completed (2022/23): 5-storey integrated facility with ASRS, cold chain, R&D
  • Blue-Chip Tenant: Paradise Group — 167 outlets, 23 years history, recent PE buyback
  • 100% Absolute Net: Tenant pays all property costs, landlord receives clean NOI
  • Attractive Yield: 6.4% vs. 4.8-5.5% for longer lease comps
  • Critical Facility: S$25M+ fit-out creates high switching costs

NEW
DD Q&A Summary (Victoria Wang, CBRE — 18 March 2026)
# Question Answer Implication
1 Why only 24 years left on lease? Paradise acquired from Tuck Lee Ice (secondary market), rebuilt facility. JTC 30-year term commenced 1 Oct 2020. No fault of vendor
2 Asset-light strategy — why now? Management pursuing asset-light to optimize capital allocation. Part of broader portfolio management initiative. Strategic, not distressed
3 JTC lease payment structure? Currently on annual land rent. Buyer pays one-time upfront JTC land premium (~S$3.9M), after which annual rent ceases. Factored into acquisition cost
4 Extension prospects? No in-built renewal option (not a 30+30). Extensions require JTC approval + additional land premium. Consents not publicly available. KEY RISK
5 Original lease term? Owner did NOT obtain land from JTC directly — purchased from secondary market with remaining tenure. No "original deal" goodwill
6 Side letters to lease? Checking with Vendor DD ITEM
7 ROFR or expansion on adjacent plots? No No organic growth option
8 Existing debt on facility? Checking with Vendor DD ITEM
9 Micro-location and planning? Well-established Defu industrial enclave. Institutionally managed + JTC assets. Stable cluster — no gentrification risk
10 Comps with sub-20 year leases? See CBRE comparables spreadsheet attached. Received ✓
⚠️ Critical Finding: No In-Built Renewal Option
"For a land lease that didn't have the in-built renewal option, any extensions will require JTC approval and requires another land premium to be paid if approved."
— Victoria Wang, CBRE (18 March 2026)

Unlike 30+30 leases where renewal is procedural, Paradise must re-apply to JTC at lease end. This is a binary risk that should be reflected in pricing.

Risk Analysis
HIGH

Tenant Concentration (Single Tenant)

100% income from Paradise Group. Default or early termination = total income loss.

Mitigation: 23-year track record, S$70-80M revenue, S$25M+ non-removable fit-out, 12-month BG, no break clause. Critical facility supplies all Singapore outlets.
MEDIUM

JTC Lease Decay / Exit Liquidity

24.5 years remaining; sub-20 year mark reduces buyer pool significantly.

Mitigation: Target Year 5 exit (19.5 yrs left, still financeable). JTC FLEXI scheme improves renewal prospects. Operator sale as backup.
MEDIUM

Cap Rate Expansion

Exit at wider cap rate than entry erodes returns.

Mitigation: Entry at 6.4% already prices in lease risk. Conservative 7.0% exit assumption. Break-even at 8.5%.
LOW

Interest Rate Risk

Floating rate debt exposes cash flow to rising rates.

Mitigation: 30% LTV limits exposure. Rate cap available. Even at +200bps, cash flow remains positive.
LOW

Bespoke Facility Re-letting

Purpose-built food facility may be hard to re-let if Paradise vacates.

Mitigation: Central kitchen demand growing (cloud kitchen boom). Other F&B operators would value turnkey facility. 10-year lease provides buffer.
