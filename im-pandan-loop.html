Understanding Singapore Industrial Real Estate
⚠️ Key Consideration for Family Office Investors: Singapore industrial real estate operates under a unique leasehold framework managed by JTC Corporation. Understanding this system is essential before investing. Below we explain why institutional investors remain comfortable with the structure despite shorter land leases.
The JTC Leasehold System

What is JTC?

JTC Corporation is Singapore's principal developer and manager of industrial infrastructure. It controls ~80% of Singapore's industrial land, leasing sites to businesses on 20-60 year terms. This government-backed system ensures orderly land use and allows periodic re-optimization of industrial zones.

Standard Lease Terms

  • New developments: 30-60 year initial lease
  • Existing buildings: 20-30 year extensions
  • Renewal criteria: P&M investment, job creation, productivity gains
  • Land rent: Paid annually to JTC (separate from building)
Why Institutional Investors Are Comfortable

✓ Predictable Framework

JTC renewal criteria are transparent and objective. Companies meeting investment thresholds (P&M, jobs, productivity) have high probability of renewal. The 2025 FLEXI scheme further eases criteria.

✓ Track Record

Historically, >90% of renewal applications from qualifying companies are approved. JTC has never forced a tenant out who met the criteria and wished to extend.

✓ Price Discovery

Active secondary market with S-REITs and institutional buyers transacting regularly. Cap rates reflect lease decay, providing transparent pricing.

✓ REIT Demand

SGX-listed industrial REITs (Mapletree, AIMS, ESR, CapitaLand Ascendas) actively acquire JTC leasehold assets, providing liquid exit options.

✓ Higher Yields

Leasehold nature results in higher going-in yields (5-6%) compared to freehold markets (3-4%), compensating for lease decay.

✓ Government Stability

Singapore's rule of law, contract enforcement, and pro-business environment minimize political/regulatory risk.

How to Generate Returns Despite Lease Decay

The key insight: Singapore industrial returns come from (1) attractive income yield, (2) rental growth, and (3) active management, not capital appreciation from land value. Investors should underwrite to a terminal value that reflects remaining lease, not perpetual ownership.

Return Drivers:
  • Income Yield (5.0-6.5%): Higher than freehold markets
  • Rental Escalations (2-3% p.a.): Built into most leases
  • AEI/Repositioning: Upgrade to higher spec = rent premium
  • Occupancy Improvement: Lease-up vacant space
Exit Strategies:
  • REIT Sale: SGX industrial REITs actively acquiring
  • Institutional Trade: Pension funds, insurance cos
  • Operator Sale: End-user purchase (less common)
  • Hold to Decay: Maximize income, accept declining value

Risk Analysis
HIGH

Tenant Concentration (Single Tenant)

100% income derived from one tenant. Default or early termination would result in total income loss and significant re-leasing costs for specialized food facility.

Mitigation: Strong tenant credit (S$200M+ revenue, 160+ outlets), 12-month rent security deposit, S$25M+ non-removable fit-out creating switching costs, 10-year lease with no break clause. Specialized facility attractive to other food operators if re-let required.
MEDIUM

JTC Lease Renewal Risk

60-year lease provides 53+ years remaining, but eventual renewal subject to JTC criteria and land premiums that may erode value.

Mitigation: Food security is government priority; FLEXI scheme (2025) eases renewal criteria. 53 years is beyond typical hold period. Exit to REIT transfers renewal risk to new owner at appropriate price.
MEDIUM

Interest Rate Risk

Floating rate debt (SORA + spread) exposes cash flow to rising rates. MAS tightening cycle could increase debt service costs.

Mitigation: Conservative 30% LTV limits exposure. Rate cap or swap available at ~0.5% cost. Even at +200bps rates, cash flow remains positive given 5.0% yield vs. ~6.7% all-in debt cost ceiling.
MEDIUM

Exit / Liquidity Risk

Limited buyer pool for specialized food industrial assets. May require price concession to exit in soft market.

Mitigation: Active REIT bid (MLT, AIMS, ESR all acquiring). Institutional demand for stable income assets. Cold chain thesis supportive of values. Alternatively, hold for income with declining terminal value.
LOW

Regulatory / Political Risk

Changes to JTC policy, land use zoning, or tax treatment could impact returns.

Mitigation: Singapore consistently ranked top for rule of law, contract enforcement, and political stability. JTC policy changes historically grandfathered for existing assets. No adverse regulatory trends identified.
LOW

Obsolescence Risk

Purpose-built food facility may become functionally obsolete as food production technology evolves.

Mitigation: 2025 completion with latest specifications. Cold chain demand growing 7% p.a. in Singapore. Generic ramp-up design allows conversion to other B2 uses if required. Tenant responsible for internal fit-out upgrades.
